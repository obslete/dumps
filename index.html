What are pollutants?

Causes:

Pollution is suspended particles. These particles, called aerosols, come in solid, liquid and gas forms. Air pollution is caused when the particles from things such as car exhaust, factory smoke, pollen and dust get suspended and are trapped in the atmosphere. Water pollution is caused from gas, oil spills, garbage, invasive bacteria and toxic chemicals enter the water via runoff from sources such as lakes, streams and drainage. Land pollution is cause by garbage and chemicals. Garbage can be pollution when it is littered on sidewalks and streets, and when it enters waterways via runoff.

Effects:

Pollution can have many effects, both on the environment and on humans. Enviromentally, it can cause birth defects in animals, kill them too, create diseases,cause algae blooms which can impact water quality and affect both animals and humans and suffocate animals that mistake pollution for food (garbage)
